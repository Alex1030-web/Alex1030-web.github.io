Pozole Verde

Pozole verde's history dates back to pre-Hispanic Aztec rituals, where the stew was originally made with human flesh and corn for ceremonial purposes. After the Spanish conquest, pork or chicken replaced the forbidden ingredients. The green (“verde”) variation comes from Guerrero, where tomatillos, cilantro, and green chiles are used.

This is a video on how to make Pozole verde if you may want to try it for you're self!

  • Ingrediants

  • Chicken
  • White Hominey
  • Chicken Broth
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Green Sauce

  • Directions

  • Simmer the meat in broth with onions, garlic, and seasonings until tender. Shred and set aside; strain the broth.
  • Roast tomatillos and peppers at 400°F for 20 minutes (or boil 5–7 minutes). Blend with cilantro, oregano, cumin, and broth. Simmer 5-10 minutes.
  • Stir the green sauce into the broth, add shredded meat and hominy, and simmer 10–20 minutes. Adjust seasoning and serve with toppings.
